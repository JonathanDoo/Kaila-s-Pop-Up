About Kaila

Greetings, my name is Kaila Dooley, a young African American woman born and raised in Compton. As you may notice, I am different from everyone else. Some know me as being quiet, especially those who knew me during my 10th–12th grade years, while others know me as the person they can count on when things go left, when someone doesn’t show up, or when help is needed. For most people, I am known as someone who never says no.

|

|

|

For those who do not really know me, I am someone who loves to cook and write, and I enjoy trying new things when they look fun or learning skills that can benefit my life and my family. I am family-oriented, deeply connected to my community, and a Christian who has been going to church since I was one year old with my mom. When COVID came around, I became scared after seeing the negative sides of people, which caused me to retreat into my shell for a period of my life. One day, I finally decided that enough was enough, and that decision led me to begin my culinary journey.

|

|

|

I started cooking because it came easily to me after receiving bad news back to back. I began cooking in my double-digit years, and it became a coping mechanism for me. At first, cooking was not my main outlet, writing was, and it still is — but over time, cooking became more of a live, laugh, and love space. I did not give up writing; I simply became more involved with cooking. While I still love writing, cooking helped me in ways I needed at t hat time.

|

|

|

As my family began telling me my food was good, I started sharing it with others. They tried it and loved it, and seeing the joy and enjoyment people got from my food encouraged me to expand my culinary skills. I began making Italian dishes, then steaks — which I jokingly call “rich people food” because of how expensive they can be. From there, I started learning American and Asian cuisines. Today, I cook because it is fun, it brings me peace of mind, and it allows me to be myself.

|

|

|

My goal is to own my own business and open restaurants around the world. I want people everywhere to experience homemade food, especially those who decide to settle and live far from where they started. Through my cooking, I hope to give people a sense of comfort, connection, and home.
