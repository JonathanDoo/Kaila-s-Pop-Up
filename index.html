Who I Are

Greetings, my name is Kaila Dooley, a young African American woman born and raised in Compton. While some know me as quiet, especially during my 10th–12th grade years, others know me as someone dependable who never says no and is always willing to help. I am family-oriented, deeply connected to my community, a Christian since the age of one, and someone who loves cooking, writing, and learning new things that benefit my life and family. After COVID caused me to retreat into my shell, I chose to step forward by beginning my culinary journey, where cooking brought me peace of mind and became the missing piece to my puzzle.

Background

I started cooking because it came easily to me, especially after receiving bad news back to back. Cooking became my safe haven, a space where I could just be myself. I began during my teenage years, I don’t remember the exact time, and it eventually transformed into a coping mechanism. Everyone has something, and at first, cooking wasn’t mine. Writing was, and it still is. However, cooking became more of a live, laugh, and love space for me. I didn’t completely let go of writing; I simply became more involved with cooking. Don’t get me wrong, I still love writing, but cooking helped me in a different way. As my family began telling me my food was good, I started sharing it with others. They tried it, loved it, and seeing the joy and enjoyment people got from my food pushed me to expand my culinary repertoire. I began making Italian dishes, then steaks, what I like to call “rich people food,” because do you know how much one costs? From there, I started learning American and Asian dishes. Now, I cook because it’s fun, and because I can truly be myself while doing it.

Future Plans

My long term goal is to own my own business and establish restaurants around the world. I want to create spaces where people from all backgrounds can experience the comfort of homemade food, especially those who choose to make a new home far from where they started. No matter where someone s in the world, I want my food to offer a sense of warmth, familiarity, and belonging. Through my restaurants, I hope to share more than just meals, I want to share culture, love, and the feeling of home. Food has the power to connect people, and my dream is to use that power to bring comfort and joy to individuals and families everywhere. Whether someone is visiting, relocating, or settling down for good, I want them to always be able to find a place that feels like home through my cooking.
